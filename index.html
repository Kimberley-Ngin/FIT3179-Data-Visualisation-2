The Black Summer: 2019-20 Australian Bushfires

Summer of 2019-20 marks one of the most catastrophic bushfire seasons recorded in Australia. This visualisation will walk you through the factors leading up to the event, as well as paint a picture of the aftermath, telling the story of how the 2019-20 bushfires devastated Australia.

______
1

Rising Temperatures

Temperature is a significant factor in fire susceptibility. Australia is continuing to experience intense heat waves and rising average annual temperatures, driving the worsening of bushfire activity.

Leading up to the 2019/20 bushfires, average annual temperatures reached an all-time high, placing 2019 as the hottest year recorded thus far.

2

Rainfall Deficiency

Risk of forest fire is further exacerbated by drier conditions and reduction in rainfall. Not only is Australia getting hotter, the country is also experiencing rainfall deficiencies in recent years. This drought is characterised by three consecutive dry winters in 2017, 2018, and 2019, particularly in the southeast and east regions of Australia.

This trend of declining annual rainfall over time further increases risk of severe fire weather.

The Worst Bushfire Season

In the summer of 2019/20, bushfires were ablaze, igniting in the north of Australia, and migrating toward the southeast regions. Over 24 million hectares of land was burned, with New South Wales experiencing its worst bushfire season.
